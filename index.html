Situated Modelling

Ontological and epistemological commitments in interdisciplinary water research

We demonstrate how research practices are not neutral but intervene in the world. Hydrology's uncertainty tradition might be an entry point for reflexion on contingencies and ethical responsibilities.

From situated knowledges to situated modelling

Situated Modelling extends a relational understanding of the world to modelling to enable a systematic interrogation of modelling choices and their consequences. We analyse the situatedness of three modelling approaches and suggest four guiding principles for Situated Modelling.

Making a case for power-sensitive water modelling

The power embedded in models is often concealed by claiming their neutrality. However, an emerging body of work offers critical insights into the political implications of hydrological models and a nuanced understanding of their application in context.

Situating Bayesian knowledge

We explore the contingency and performativity of knowing in Bayesian ways by revisiting a case of pollutant transfers from land to water. We query this material from an STS perspective and discuss how Bayesian modelling practices enact aspects of the world and can (re)configure (social) relations.

Situating hydrological modeling

We explore how the power of models can be explicitly and constructively engaged with, thereby expanding their potential to support transformations to water justice and sustainability.

Modelling water worlds

We present three complementary entry points for analysing models and modelling practices. Models not only contribute to reproducing water inequalities but that they can also be mobilised to understand and address them.
